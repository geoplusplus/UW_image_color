Spot white-balance with extreem color-cast

by olegk » 6th Oct 2014, Mon, 10:57

I'm trying to use the spot-WB feature on an underwater image of a gray target taken at more than 20m depth. There's very little red there, so I don't expect wonders.
It is trivial to do in Corel AfterShot - the colors are reasonable: http://www.lazyconv.com/galleries/DEMO/ ... erShot.jpg
But RawTherapee produces weird red cast; here's the best I was able to achieve: http://www.lazyconv.com/galleries/DEMO/ ... erapee.jpg
In any converter I had to set black-point to minimum in order to get at least some red. In AfterShot it's a single setting and it works as expected. In RawTherapee I reduced to minimum all 3 per-color black-points in the "RAW" tab.
I tried different sample sizes - same result. While the color under the "probe" does get more or less neutral, the rest of the image goes badly red.

Am I doing anything wrong?

Here are more relevant files:
- RAW image itself: http://www.lazyconv.com/galleries/DEMO/ ... C03356.ARW
- RawTherapee settings file: http://www.lazyconv.com/galleries/DEMO/ ... 56.ARW.pp3
- Aftershot settings file: http://www.lazyconv.com/galleries/DEMO/ ... 56.ARW.xmp

Thank you in advance,
Oleg.

Re: Spot white-balance with extreem color-cast

by ion12 » 6th Oct 2014, Mon, 21:17

Since as you say there is very little red, I would start with a neutral profile and then use the RGB curves to remap the red channel - looking at the histogram and the rgb values in the navigator - until in the neutral color areas the green and red are more or less equalized. Then tweak the blue channel curve to set how much of the blue cast to keep. Further RGB curves tweaks, tone/luminance curve, saturation etc. to taste. Noise reduction is must because red channel is an extreme case and will introduce a lot of ugly red noise.
That's how I would approach it.

Re: Spot white-balance with extreem color-cast

by iliasG » 7th Oct 2014, Tue, 02:02

RT's WB calculations fall out of range on such extreme WB.

There are two underwater WB presets in RT ( as I understand it underwater1 is adequate for small depths, and underwater2 deeper) where one can use the preset as a starting point and then do either auto WB or spotWB. Basically underwater presets make use of the third slider "blue/red equalizer" to bring calculations in range. This slider has to be utilized in extra cold or extra warm WB conditions (shift to red or blue).

For your specific shot it looks like the range of the slider to red is not enough so may be an expansion to around 2.0 is desirable. For the moment you can put it to 1.5 and then play lowering the tint to magenta. If it is not enough increase the red in channel mixer. The best I could manage was with temp 60000, tint 0.26, blue/red 1.50, red 140 at channel mixer ..

Re: Spot white-balance with extreem color-cast

by iliasG » 7th Oct 2014, Tue, 16:05

Don't touch black levels for this. There is almost no signal below black level just noise, you just inject a color cast in the darks by doing this and destroy contrast.
Only if you see excessive green noise at the dark by lowering the BL you can have a benefit (basically this way you are correcting a possible wrong BL of NEX-C3's metadata) and then a small change is needed (at most -10).

In a second inspection I detected something in RT's internals
The problem here with NEX-C3 is that RT uses a custom color matrix (by Colin Walker) which is probably under warmer lighting (D50 ??) than the usual D65 used for WB calculations.

Try with adding in your camconst.json file (just after NEX-5N lines) the following lines

Code: Select all
    { // Quality A, correction for color matrix from Colin Walker's d50 to dng d65
        "make_model": "Sony NEX-C3",
      //  dcraw_matrix": [ 5130,-1055,-269,-4473,11797,3050,-701,1310,7121 ], // RT's internal by Colin Walker
        "dcraw_matrix": [ 5991,-1456,-455,-4764,12135,2980,-707,1425,6701 ], // Dcraw default D65
        "ranges": { "black": 512, "white": 16300 }
    },


Then the WB do not fail that much and you just need to put b/r at 1.50 and autoWB or pickWB .. maybe a slight calibration with channel mixer is needed .. G=97 B=103
